Brownian Motion (1D Random Walk)

Setup

Let $X_t$ be a symmetric random walk starting at $X_0 = 5$ with absorbing barriers at $0$ and $10$.

Mathematical Proof

For a symmetric random walk with equal step probabilities $p = q = 0.5$:

$$P(\text{reach 10 before 0}) = \frac{\text{starting position} - 0}{10 - 0} = \frac{5 - 0}{10 - 0} = \frac{5}{10} = 0.5$$

By symmetry:

$$P(\text{reach 0 before 10}) = 1 - P(\text{reach 10 before 0}) = 1 - 0.5 = 0.5$$
Result: Exactly 50% chance of success, 50% chance of failure ✓

2D Random Walk

Setup

Starting at $(5,5)$ in a $[0,10] \times [0,10]$ grid with:

  • Success: Reach right edge ($x \geq 10$) OR top edge ($y \geq 10$)
  • Failure: Reach left edge ($x \leq 0$) OR bottom edge ($y \leq 0$)

Mathematical Proof

Each step moves in one of 4 directions with equal probability $\frac{1}{4}$:

  • Up: $(x, y+0.5)$ — moves toward success region
  • Right: $(x+0.5, y)$ — moves toward success region
  • Down: $(x, y-0.5)$ — moves toward failure region
  • Left: $(x-0.5, y)$ — moves toward failure region

By perfect symmetry of the setup:

$$P(\text{success}) = P(\text{failure}) = 0.5$$
Result: Exactly 50% chance of success, 50% chance of failure ✓

Poisson Process

Setup

We want a Poisson process with rate $\lambda$ such that in $T = 10$ seconds:

$$P[N(10) \geq 10] = P[N(10) \leq 9] = 0.5$$

Mathematical Derivation

Let $N(10) \sim \text{Poisson}(\mu)$ where $\mu = \lambda T = 10\lambda$.

We solve:

$$F_{\text{Poisson}(\mu)}(9) = 0.5$$

Using the cumulative distribution function of Poisson distribution, this yields:

$$\mu \approx 9.6687146$$

Therefore:

$$\lambda = \frac{\mu}{T} \approx \frac{9.6687146}{10} \approx 0.96687146 \text{ s}^{-1}$$

Verification

With $\mu \approx 9.6687$:

  • $P[N(10) \leq 9] = F_{\text{Poisson}(9.6687)}(9) = 0.5$
  • $P[N(10) \geq 10] = 1 - F_{\text{Poisson}(9.6687)}(9) = 0.5$
Result: Exactly 50% chance of success, 50% chance of failure ✓

Symmetric graph structure:

Red Terminal ↔ Left Cycle (20 nodes) ↔ Left Bridge ↔ Center ↔ Right Bridge ↔ Right Cycle (20 nodes) ↔ Green Terminal

Mathematical Proof

By the symmetry principle for random walks on graphs:

  1. Structural Symmetry: The graph is perfectly symmetric with respect to the center node
  2. Path Length Equivalence: The expected path length from center to red terminal equals the expected path length from center to green terminal
  3. Transition Probabilities: At each node, the random walk chooses among connected nodes with equal probability

For a symmetric random walk on a symmetric graph starting at the center:

$$P(\text{reach red terminal}) = P(\text{reach green terminal}) = 0.5$$
